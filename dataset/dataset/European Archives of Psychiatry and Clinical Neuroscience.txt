European Archives of Psychiatry and Clinical Neuroscience is a peer-reviewed medical journal published 8 times a year by Springer Science+Business Media.

History 
The journal was established in 1868 by the German neurologist and psychiatrist Wilhelm Griesinger as the Archiv für Psychiatrie und Nervenkrankheiten (English: Archives of Psychiatry and Nerve Diseases. From 1869, its editors-in-chief were Bernhard von Gudden and Carl Friedrich Otto Westphal. From 1984 to 1989, the journal was named European Archives of Psychiatry and Neurological Sciences, before obtaining its current title in 1990. In 1947, after a publication hiatus since 1944, the journal merged with the Zeitschrift für die gesamte Neurologie und Psychiatrie, which had been published independently since 1910. The merged journal continued the volume numbering of the latter journal.
